Overview

Mental Health is important to us because it affects the majority of college students. Especially given the pandemic, students are feeling isolated and struggling to properly care for their mental well being. Low mental health can lead to poor physical health, lower grades, and has many other negative effects. When we support each other and are open about our struggles, we as a community can overcome many challenges we face. We organized this music festival and concert to improve the mental health of students at UMD who are struggling to cope with the pandemic.

Venues

Adele Stamp Union
Mckeldin Mall

The Adele Stamp Union will feature music from artists who speak up about mental health. Smaller stations and booths will offer information, resources, mindfulness activities, as well as merchandise whose proceeds will be donated to mental health research and advocacy groups. Specific stations at Mckeldin will offer art therapy, with freestyle music and painting, or an event led to teach students how to play certain instruments therapeutically. It will span multiple days to ensure people who come can remain 6 feet apart. People can pick a day to show up, and days will max out after a certain number of reservations to keep the numbers safe. ALL COVID-19 protocols must be followed or the guest will be asked to leave.
